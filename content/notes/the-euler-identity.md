Euler's Identity: e^(iπ) + 1 = 0

Often called the most beautiful equation in mathematics,
Euler's identity connects five fundamental constants:

  e  — Euler's number (2.71828...), base of natural logarithms
  i  — The imaginary unit, where i² = -1
  π  — Pi (3.14159...), the ratio of a circle's circumference
       to its diameter
  1  — The multiplicative identity
  0  — The additive identity

The identity is a special case of Euler's formula:

  e^(ix) = cos(x) + i·sin(x)

When x = π, we get:
  e^(iπ) = cos(π) + i·sin(π)
  e^(iπ) = -1 + 0
  e^(iπ) + 1 = 0

This elegant result is often the first glimpse into the
profound unity of mathematics for many students.
