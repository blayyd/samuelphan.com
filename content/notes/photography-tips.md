Quick Photography Tips
=====================

1. The Rule of Thirds
   Imagine a 3x3 grid over your frame. Place key subjects
   along the lines or at their intersections for a more
   balanced composition.

2. Natural Light is Your Friend
   The golden hours (just after sunrise and before sunset)
   provide warm, diffused light that flatters most subjects.
   Avoid harsh midday sun when shadows are too strong.

3. Get Closer
   Fill the frame with your subject. Most beginning
   photographers leave too much empty space around
   their subject.

4. Keep the Horizon Level
   A tilted horizon is distracting. Use your camera's
   grid overlay to keep it straight.

5. Experiment with Angles
   Don't just shoot from eye level. Try crouching down,
   climbing up, or tilting the camera for more dynamic
   perspectives.

6. Focus on the Eyes
   For portraits, the eyes should always be in sharp
   focus. They are the most important element.

Equipment:
- Any camera is better than no camera
- A 50mm f/1.8 lens is the best value in photography
- A tripod unlocks long exposures and sharper images

Remember: the best camera is the one you have with you.
